Re-design of Charity Careers Canada Website

CharityCareersCanada is a Toronto-based, nonprofit platform that serves as a centralized resource for individuals interested in pursuing careers in the nonprofit and charitable sectors in Canada.
It helps job seekers find job opportunities while providing valuable resources and support to aid in their career development within these sectors.
Additionally, it benefits nonprofit organizations by connecting them with individuals who share their commitment to social and environmental causes.

Problem Statement:

The current website, as is, consists of many problems ranging from design, user interface, usability, and functionality. The concept is solid, but through user testing, and our own assessments, the website in its current form is borderline broken webpage to the users.

Project Goals:

  • Enhance User Experience: The current website, as is, consists of many problems ranging from design, user interface, usability, and functionality. The concept is solid, but through user testing, and our own assessments, the website in its current form is borderline broken webpage to the users.
  • Increase User Engagement: Increase user engagement by providing valuable content, such as articles, guides, and resources related to nonprofit job searching and career development.
  • Expand Job Listings: Increase the number of job listings on the website by attracting more charities and organizations to post their job openings.
  • Improve Search and Filtering: Increase the number of job listings on the website by attracting more charities and organizations to post their job openings.

User Interviews:

We conducted some user interviews with the existing website. The website has many issues like some links leading to the 401 page not found error page. The UI design was not pleasing as it had very bright red colors which users complained of as symbolizing a danger sign. The navigation was not user-friendly to get the information they wanted. Some navigation leads to a broken webpage. The heuristic checklist was created to list down all evaluate the current state of the website and what were the challenges and issues that need to be focused.

Based on 5 interviews and Google form surveys, I created an affinity diagram with all the valuable inputs from users. There are some repeated experiences shared which are combined and put under specific categories.

Affinity Diagram:

An affinity diagram was used to bring all the challenges, suggestions, and feedback received from the user interviews conducted. It was categorized and analyzed based on the different scopes.

Feature Prioritization Matrix:

The matrix helps to sort out the importance of the website and the complexity involved. Employer's and job seeker's suggestions were analyzed like job listings made easy. The convenience of applying for jobs directly from the site, and accessing blogs to learn from experiences from others. Considering the employers for all the job listings with any current openings were included as well.

Prototype:

The final prototype was created considering all the users pain points like the bright red colors were changed, and easy to navigate. Different tabs for Jobs and Volunteer opportunities were listed and filter options to get specific job/volunteer descriptions. Resumes can be uploaded and sent to the employers from the website with a confirmation email sent.

Final Thoughts:

I have learned a lot from user testing, and there is always some valuable feedback that will refine the app.
Planning and organizing the ideas, analyzing user insights, and always connecting back to the problem statement helped me in the design process.
There is always more room for learning. The next step would be to add a feedback feature in the app to get any ideas, improvements, ratings, or issues users have.
